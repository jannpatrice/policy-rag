# POL-002 Leave and Time-Off Policy

**Document ID:** POL-002
**Document Title:** Leave and Time-Off Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture Department

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the rules governing employee leave entitlements, leave applications, approvals, and time-off management at InterCloud Solutions Philippines, Inc.

The company recognizes the importance of work-life balance and provides leave benefits to support employee well-being.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all regular and probationary employees unless otherwise specified by employment contract or applicable law.

---

# 3. Leave Principles

Employees are encouraged to:

* Take planned time off for rest and recovery.
* Schedule leave responsibly.
* Minimize disruption to project and client commitments.
* Provide advance notice whenever possible.

Leave benefits are not intended to replace attendance responsibilities.

---

# 4. Vacation Leave (VL)

## Entitlement

Regular employees are entitled to fifteen (15) Vacation Leave days per calendar year.

Vacation Leave is intended for:

* Personal time off
* Family activities
* Rest and recreation
* Personal appointments

## Accrual

Vacation Leave is accrued monthly throughout the calendar year.

## Usage

Employees may take Vacation Leave in:

* Full-day increments
* Half-day increments

Manager approval is required before leave is considered approved.

---

# 5. Sick Leave (SL)

## Entitlement

Regular employees are entitled to ten (10) Sick Leave days per calendar year.

## Acceptable Uses

Sick Leave may be used for:

* Personal illness
* Medical appointments
* Recovery from injury
* Mental health and wellness needs

## Medical Certificates

A medical certificate may be required when:

* Sick Leave exceeds two (2) consecutive working days.
* Patterns of excessive absenteeism are observed.
* Management determines additional documentation is necessary.

---

# 6. Probationary Employee Leave

Probationary employees accrue leave benefits during their probationary period.

Unused accrued leave may be utilized subject to manager approval.

The company reserves the right to limit leave usage during the first ninety (90) days of employment except for emergency circumstances.

---

# 7. Leave Application Process

## Planned Leave

Employees should submit leave requests at least:

* Three (3) working days in advance for leave lasting one day or less.
* Five (5) working days in advance for leave lasting more than one day.

## Emergency Leave

Employees should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible.

Notification may be made through:

* Company messaging platforms
* Email
* Phone call
* SMS

---

# 8. Leave Approval

Managers are responsible for balancing:

* Employee well-being
* Team coverage
* Client commitments
* Operational requirements

Managers may decline leave requests when:

* Critical project deadlines exist.
* Insufficient team coverage is available.
* Multiple team members have overlapping leave requests.

Managers should provide reasonable justification when declining requests.

---

# 9. Vacation Leave Carryover

Employees may carry over up to five (5) unused Vacation Leave days into the following calendar year.

Carryover leave must be used by March 31 of the following year.

Unused carryover leave expires after the deadline.

No cash conversion is provided for expired leave unless required by law.

---

# 10. Sick Leave Carryover

Unused Sick Leave does not carry over to the following year.

Unused Sick Leave has no cash value and expires at year-end.

---

# 11. Bereavement Leave

Regular employees are entitled to three (3) paid working days of Bereavement Leave upon the death of an immediate family member.

Immediate family members include:

* Spouse
* Parent
* Child
* Sibling
* Legal guardian

Additional leave may be approved at management discretion.

---

# 12. Maternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to maternity leave benefits in accordance with applicable Philippine laws and regulations.

The company will comply with all legally mandated maternity leave requirements.

Employees should coordinate with People & Culture regarding documentation requirements.

---

# 13. Paternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to paternity leave benefits in accordance with applicable Philippine laws and regulations.

Supporting documentation may be required.

---

# 14. Solo Parent Leave

Qualified employees may avail of Solo Parent Leave in accordance with applicable Philippine laws and regulations.

Employees must provide documentation establishing eligibility.

---

# 15. Study Leave

Employees approved for certification exams, academic programs, or professional development activities may be granted Study Leave subject to management approval.

Additional guidelines are provided in POL-011 Learning and Development Policy.

---

# 16. Unpaid Leave

Employees who have exhausted available leave credits may request unpaid leave.

Approval is not guaranteed and will be evaluated based on:

* Business requirements
* Employee performance
* Duration of absence
* Team coverage considerations

---

# 17. Public Holidays

The company observes Philippine public holidays as declared by the government.

Employees required to work during a holiday may be eligible for additional compensation or offset arrangements subject to company guidelines.

---

# 18. Leave During Performance Improvement Plans

Employees on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) remain eligible for leave benefits.

However, excessive leave utilization that impacts the employee's ability to complete agreed objectives may be considered during performance evaluations.

---

# 19. Leave Misrepresentation

Providing false information regarding leave usage, medical conditions, or supporting documentation constitutes misconduct.

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

---

# 20. Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require approval from:

* Department Head
* People & Culture Department

All approved exceptions must be documented.
