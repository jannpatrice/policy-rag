# POL-003 Remote Work Policy

**Document ID:** POL-003
**Document Title:** Remote Work Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Operations Department

---

# 1. Purpose

InterCloud Solutions Philippines, Inc. operates under a remote-first working model. This policy establishes expectations, responsibilities, and requirements for employees performing work outside company offices.

The objective of this policy is to maintain productivity, security, collaboration, and employee well-being while supporting workplace flexibility.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees authorized to perform work remotely.

Certain client engagements may require onsite attendance and may be subject to additional requirements.

---

# 3. Remote-First Philosophy

The company evaluates employees based on:

* Results
* Accountability
* Collaboration
* Client outcomes

Employees are not evaluated based on physical presence alone.

Remote work is considered a standard operating model rather than a special privilege.

---

# 4. Approved Work Locations

Employees may work from:

* Their primary residence
* A company-approved coworking space
* A temporary domestic location approved by their manager

Employees must ensure their work environment is:

* Safe
* Professional
* Secure
* Suitable for confidential work

---

# 5. Work Outside the Philippines

Employees may request temporary work outside the Philippines for up to thirty (30) calendar days per year.

Approval is required from:

* Immediate Manager
* Department Head

Requests exceeding thirty (30) days require executive approval.

The company may deny requests due to:

* Client obligations
* Data security concerns
* Legal or tax considerations
* Operational requirements

---

# 6. Work Schedule Expectations

Employees are expected to maintain agreed working hours.

Core collaboration hours are:

* 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM Philippine Time

Employees should remain available during core collaboration hours unless on approved leave or attending external engagements.

---

# 7. Communication Standards

Employees must actively use approved communication tools including:

* Microsoft Teams
* Company Email
* Project Management Platforms

Employees are expected to:

* Respond to internal communications within a reasonable timeframe.
* Attend scheduled meetings.
* Maintain accurate calendar availability.

---

# 8. Attendance at Meetings

Employees must attend:

* Team meetings
* Client meetings
* Company-wide town halls
* Performance discussions

Absences should be communicated in advance whenever possible.

---

# 9. Internet Connectivity Requirements

Employees are responsible for maintaining reliable internet access suitable for their role.

Minimum recommended internet speed:

* 50 Mbps download
* 20 Mbps upload

Employees should maintain a backup connectivity option whenever practical.

Examples include:

* Mobile hotspot
* Secondary ISP
* Coworking facility access

---

# 10. Internet Allowance

Regular employees receive a monthly internet allowance of ₱1,500.

The allowance is intended to offset internet service costs associated with remote work.

The allowance is subject to payroll processing and applicable deductions where required.

---

# 11. Workspace Requirements

Employees should maintain a workspace that:

* Supports focused work
* Minimizes excessive noise
* Protects confidential information
* Allows professional participation in meetings

Employees are responsible for maintaining an appropriate working environment.

---

# 12. Information Security Requirements

Employees working remotely must:

* Use company-approved devices.
* Enable multi-factor authentication.
* Lock devices when unattended.
* Avoid sharing company devices.
* Protect client information.

Additional requirements are detailed in POL-004 Information Security Policy.

---

# 13. Equipment Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for:

* Company-issued laptops
* Monitors
* Accessories
* Security tokens

Lost or damaged equipment must be reported within twenty-four (24) hours.

Additional requirements are detailed in POL-008 Equipment & Asset Policy.

---

# 14. Onsite Attendance

Employees may be required to attend:

* Client workshops
* Strategic planning sessions
* Team-building events
* Training programs
* Company meetings

Reasonable notice should be provided whenever possible.

---

# 15. Travel Requirements

When travel is required for business purposes, employees must comply with POL-007 Travel Policy.

Travel-related expenses may be reimbursed subject to policy requirements.

---

# 16. Health and Safety

Employees are encouraged to:

* Take regular breaks
* Maintain ergonomic workstations
* Report work-related injuries promptly
* Manage workload responsibly

Remote employees remain responsible for maintaining a safe working environment.

---

# 17. Performance Expectations

Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as onsite employees.

Managers may evaluate:

* Quality of work
* Timeliness
* Client satisfaction
* Collaboration
* Communication effectiveness

---

# 18. Remote Work Misconduct

Examples of misconduct include:

* Falsifying attendance records
* Unauthorized secondary employment during working hours
* Sharing company credentials
* Performing work from unapproved locations when approval is required

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

---

# 19. Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require approval from:

* Department Head
* Operations Department

All exceptions must be documented.
