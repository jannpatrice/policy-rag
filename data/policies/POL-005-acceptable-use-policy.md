# POL-005 Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** POL-005
**Document Title:** Acceptable Use Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Information Security Department

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish acceptable and responsible use of InterCloud Solutions Philippines, Inc. technology resources.

Technology resources are provided to support business operations, client service delivery, collaboration, and professional development.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Employees
* Contractors
* Interns
* Consultants
* Third parties authorized to use company systems

The policy covers all company-owned and company-managed technology resources.

---

# 3. Covered Resources

This policy applies to:

* Laptops
* Mobile devices
* Email systems
* Collaboration platforms
* Cloud services
* Source code repositories
* Internal applications
* Internet access
* Company networks

---

# 4. Business Use Expectations

Company resources are provided primarily for business purposes.

Employees are expected to use technology resources responsibly and professionally.

Incidental personal use is permitted provided it:

* Does not interfere with work responsibilities.
* Does not consume excessive resources.
* Does not violate company policies.
* Does not create security risks.

---

# 5. Prohibited Activities

Employees may not use company systems to:

* Engage in illegal activities.
* Harass or discriminate against others.
* Distribute malicious software.
* Access unauthorized systems.
* Conduct personal commercial activities.
* Store or distribute offensive content.
* Circumvent security controls.

Any activity that damages company reputation or client trust is prohibited.

---

# 6. Software Installation

Only approved software may be installed on company devices.

Employees must not:

* Install pirated software.
* Disable security software.
* Install unlicensed applications.
* Modify operating system security settings without authorization.

Requests for additional software should be submitted through the approved IT process.

---

# 7. Internet Usage

Employees may use company internet resources for legitimate business purposes.

Limited personal browsing is permitted provided it:

* Does not impact productivity.
* Does not violate company policies.
* Does not introduce security risks.

Employees may not access:

* Illegal content
* Malicious websites
* Pirated software repositories
* Unauthorized file-sharing services

---

# 8. Email Usage

Company email accounts should be used primarily for business communications.

Employees must:

* Maintain professional communication standards.
* Protect confidential information.
* Verify unusual requests before responding.

Employees may not:

* Send spam.
* Distribute malware.
* Share sensitive information without authorization.

---

# 9. Collaboration Platforms

Employees are expected to use approved collaboration tools responsibly.

Examples include:

* Microsoft Teams
* Outlook
* SharePoint
* Approved project management systems

Business communications conducted through company platforms may be retained for operational, legal, or compliance purposes.

---

# 10. Source Code and Intellectual Property

Employees must protect company and client intellectual property.

Employees may not:

* Copy source code to personal repositories.
* Share client code without authorization.
* Remove proprietary notices.
* Use company intellectual property for personal gain.

All work created within the scope of employment remains the property of InterCloud Solutions unless otherwise specified by contract.

---

# 11. Artificial Intelligence Tools

The company recognizes the value of AI-assisted productivity.

Employees may use approved AI tools for:

* Draft generation
* Research assistance
* Code assistance
* Documentation support

Employees must review all AI-generated outputs before use.

Employees may not submit the following into public AI systems:

* Client confidential information
* Production credentials
* Restricted company information
* Personal employee records
* Regulated client data

When uncertainty exists, employees should seek guidance from Information Security.

---

# 12. External Storage Devices

Use of external storage devices should be limited.

Employees must not connect unauthorized devices to company systems.

Approved devices may be encrypted as required by Information Security.

---

# 13. Personal Devices

Use of personal devices for company work may be permitted when approved by management and Information Security.

Approved personal devices must comply with security requirements including:

* Device encryption
* Screen lock
* Anti-malware protection
* Operating system updates

---

# 14. Data Storage

Employees should store business information only in approved company systems.

Employees may not:

* Store company files in personal cloud accounts.
* Transfer company information to unauthorized platforms.
* Maintain local copies of confidential data longer than necessary.

---

# 15. Social Media Usage

Employees should exercise professionalism when discussing company-related matters online.

Employees may not:

* Represent themselves as official company spokespersons without authorization.
* Disclose confidential information.
* Share non-public client information.

Personal opinions should not be presented as company positions.

---

# 16. Monitoring

The company reserves the right to monitor the use of company systems for:

* Security purposes
* Operational purposes
* Compliance requirements
* Incident investigations

Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company-owned resources.

---

# 17. Reporting Concerns

Employees should report:

* Security concerns
* Policy violations
* Unauthorized software
* Suspicious activity

Reports may be submitted to:

* Information Security
* IT Support
* Human Resources
* Management

---

# 18. Policy Violations

Examples of violations include:

* Installing unauthorized software
* Sharing credentials
* Copying confidential information
* Using company resources for unauthorized business activities

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

---

# 19. Exceptions

Exceptions require written approval from:

* Information Security Manager
* Department Head

Approved exceptions must be documented.
