# POL-009 Performance Management Policy

**Document ID:** POL-009
**Document Title:** Performance Management Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture Department

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a fair, transparent, and consistent framework for evaluating employee performance, supporting professional growth, recognizing achievement, and addressing performance concerns.

Performance management is intended to help employees succeed and contribute meaningfully to company objectives.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all regular and probationary employees.

Contractors and consultants may be evaluated under separate contractual arrangements.

---

# 3. Performance Philosophy

InterCloud Solutions evaluates employees based on:

* Results achieved
* Quality of work
* Professional behavior
* Collaboration
* Client impact
* Alignment with company values

Performance management is intended to support development rather than solely evaluate outcomes.

---

# 4. Probationary Employment

New employees are generally subject to a probationary period of up to six (6) months.

During probation, employees are evaluated on:

* Technical competence
* Communication
* Reliability
* Collaboration
* Cultural fit

Managers are expected to provide feedback throughout the probationary period.

---

# 5. Probation Review

A formal probation review shall occur before the end of the probationary period.

Possible outcomes include:

## Regularization

Employee successfully meets expectations.

## Probation Extension

An extension of up to three (3) months may be granted when additional evaluation is required and permitted by applicable laws.

## Separation

Employee fails to meet minimum performance expectations.

---

# 6. Performance Review Cycle

The company conducts formal performance reviews twice annually.

Review periods include:

* Mid-Year Review
* Annual Review

Managers may conduct additional reviews as needed.

---

# 7. Performance Criteria

Performance evaluations may consider:

## Delivery and Results

Achievement of agreed objectives and commitments.

## Quality

Accuracy, completeness, and professionalism of work.

## Collaboration

Ability to work effectively with colleagues and clients.

## Communication

Clarity, responsiveness, and professionalism.

## Leadership

Applicable to employees with leadership responsibilities.

## Continuous Learning

Investment in professional development and skill growth.

---

# 8. Goal Setting

Employees and managers should establish measurable objectives.

Goals should be:

* Specific
* Measurable
* Achievable
* Relevant
* Time-bound

Goals may be adjusted during the review period when business needs change.

---

# 9. Performance Ratings

The company uses the following performance ratings:

## Exceptional

Consistently exceeds expectations and delivers outstanding results.

## Exceeds Expectations

Frequently performs above expected standards.

## Meets Expectations

Successfully fulfills role requirements.

## Needs Improvement

Performance falls below expected standards in one or more areas.

## Unsatisfactory

Performance significantly fails to meet role expectations.

---

# 10. Feedback and Coaching

Managers are expected to provide ongoing feedback throughout the year.

Feedback should be:

* Timely
* Constructive
* Specific
* Actionable

Employees are encouraged to actively seek feedback and discuss development opportunities.

---

# 11. Development Planning

Employees may work with managers to create development plans focused on:

* Technical skills
* Leadership capabilities
* Certifications
* Career growth objectives

Development activities may be supported through POL-011 Learning and Development Policy.

---

# 12. Promotions

Promotion decisions may consider:

* Sustained performance
* Business needs
* Role readiness
* Leadership potential
* Skill development

Promotion is not automatically tied to tenure.

---

# 13. Compensation Adjustments

Compensation adjustments may consider:

* Performance results
* Market conditions
* Business performance
* Internal equity

Salary adjustments are not guaranteed.

---

# 14. Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

Employees whose performance falls below expectations may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan.

The objective of a PIP is to provide a structured opportunity for improvement.

---

# 15. PIP Requirements

A Performance Improvement Plan should include:

* Specific performance concerns
* Expected outcomes
* Success criteria
* Timeline for improvement
* Manager support commitments

Employees are expected to actively participate in the improvement process.

---

# 16. PIP Duration

A typical Performance Improvement Plan lasts thirty (30) calendar days.

The company may extend a PIP when additional evaluation is required.

Extensions are generally limited to an additional thirty (30) calendar days.

---

# 17. PIP Outcomes

Possible outcomes include:

## Successful Completion

Employee demonstrates sustained improvement.

## PIP Extension

Additional evaluation period is required.

## Separation

Employee fails to demonstrate required improvement.

---

# 18. Employee Responsibilities

Employees are expected to:

* Perform assigned duties responsibly.
* Participate in review discussions.
* Pursue continuous improvement.
* Act on feedback constructively.

---

# 19. Manager Responsibilities

Managers are expected to:

* Set clear expectations.
* Provide regular feedback.
* Conduct fair evaluations.
* Support employee development.

---

# 20. Appeals

Employees who believe a performance evaluation is inaccurate may discuss concerns with:

* Their Manager
* Department Head
* People & Culture Department

Appeals should be submitted within ten (10) working days of receiving the evaluation.

---

# 21. Policy Violations

Falsification of performance information, evaluation records, or supporting documentation may result in disciplinary action.
