# POL-004 Information Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-004
**Document Title:** Information Security Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Information Security Department

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets belonging to InterCloud Solutions Philippines, Inc., its employees, partners, and clients.

All employees share responsibility for maintaining information security.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Employees
* Contractors
* Interns
* Consultants
* Third parties granted access to company systems

The policy covers all company-managed information and technology assets.

---

# 3. Security Principles

The company follows these principles:

* Least Privilege Access
* Need-to-Know Access
* Defense in Depth
* Secure by Default
* Continuous Monitoring

Access to information should be limited to what is necessary for business purposes.

---

# 4. Data Classification

Information shall be classified into one of the following categories:

## Public

Information approved for public release.

Examples:

* Marketing materials
* Public website content
* Published case studies

## Internal

Information intended for internal use only.

Examples:

* Internal procedures
* Team documentation
* Internal announcements

## Confidential

Information that could negatively impact the company or clients if disclosed.

Examples:

* Financial information
* Client project information
* Employee records

## Restricted

Highly sensitive information requiring enhanced protection.

Examples:

* Production credentials
* Encryption keys
* Security investigations
* Client-regulated data

---

# 5. Password Requirements

Passwords must:

* Contain at least twelve (12) characters.
* Include upper and lowercase letters.
* Include at least one number.
* Include at least one special character.

Employees must not:

* Reuse company passwords on personal services.
* Share passwords with others.
* Store passwords in unsecured locations.

The use of a password manager is strongly encouraged.

---

# 6. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for:

* Email accounts
* VPN access
* Administrative systems
* Source code repositories
* Cloud platforms

Employees may not disable MFA without written authorization from Information Security.

---

# 7. Access Management

Access rights must be granted according to business requirements.

Managers are responsible for approving access requests.

Access reviews shall be conducted at least annually.

Access must be removed promptly when:

* Employment ends
* Job responsibilities change
* Access is no longer required

---

# 8. Acceptable Use of Company Systems

Company systems must be used primarily for legitimate business purposes.

Employees may not:

* Install unauthorized software.
* Circumvent security controls.
* Access illegal content.
* Attempt unauthorized system access.

Additional requirements are detailed in POL-005 Acceptable Use Policy.

---

# 9. Device Security

Employees must:

* Use company-approved devices.
* Maintain operating system updates.
* Enable disk encryption.
* Enable screen lock functionality.

Devices should automatically lock after fifteen (15) minutes of inactivity.

---

# 10. Endpoint Protection

Company devices must maintain:

* Approved antivirus software
* Endpoint detection and response software
* Security updates
* Device management enrollment

Employees may not remove security software without authorization.

---

# 11. Remote Work Security

Employees working remotely must:

* Use secure internet connections.
* Protect devices from unauthorized access.
* Avoid discussing confidential information in public settings.
* Lock screens when away from workstations.

Remote work requirements are further detailed in POL-003 Remote Work Policy.

---

# 12. Email Security

Employees should exercise caution when handling emails.

Employees must:

* Verify suspicious requests.
* Avoid opening unknown attachments.
* Report phishing attempts.

The Information Security team may conduct phishing awareness exercises periodically.

---

# 13. Client Data Protection

Client information must be handled according to contractual obligations and applicable regulations.

Employees may not:

* Transfer client data to personal accounts.
* Store client data on unauthorized systems.
* Share client data without authorization.

Client confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment.

---

# 14. Artificial Intelligence Tools

Employees may use approved AI tools for business purposes.

Employees may not enter:

* Client confidential information
* Restricted company information
* Credentials
* Personally identifiable information

into public AI systems unless explicitly authorized.

---

# 15. Security Incident Reporting

Employees must immediately report:

* Lost devices
* Credential compromise
* Phishing attacks
* Malware infections
* Unauthorized access attempts
* Data breaches

Incidents should be reported within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery.

---

# 16. Lost or Stolen Devices

Employees must report lost or stolen devices immediately.

The report should include:

* Device type
* Last known location
* Approximate time of loss
* Data potentially affected

The company may remotely lock or wipe affected devices.

---

# 17. Security Awareness Training

Employees are required to complete annual security awareness training.

Additional training may be assigned based on role or business requirements.

Failure to complete mandatory training may result in suspension of system access.

---

# 18. Monitoring and Auditing

The company reserves the right to monitor:

* Company devices
* Corporate networks
* Business systems
* Security events

Monitoring activities will be conducted in accordance with applicable laws and business requirements.

Employees should not expect privacy when using company-owned systems.

---

# 19. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:

* Additional training
* Corrective action
* Suspension of access
* Termination of employment

The severity of action will depend on the nature and impact of the violation.

---

# 20. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require written approval from:

* Information Security Manager
* Relevant Department Head

All approved exceptions must be documented.
