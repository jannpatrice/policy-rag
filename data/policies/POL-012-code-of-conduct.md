# POL-012 Code of Conduct

**Document ID:** POL-012
**Document Title:** Code of Conduct
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture Department

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to establish the standards of professional and ethical behavior expected of all employees, contractors, and representatives of InterCloud Solutions Philippines, Inc.

The company is committed to maintaining a workplace founded on integrity, professionalism, accountability, respect, and trust.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Employees
* Managers
* Executives
* Contractors
* Interns
* Consultants

The policy applies during:

* Working hours
* Business travel
* Client engagements
* Company-sponsored events
* Interactions conducted on behalf of the company

---

# 3. Core Principles

Employees are expected to:

* Act with integrity.
* Treat others with respect.
* Protect confidential information.
* Comply with laws and regulations.
* Avoid conflicts of interest.
* Represent the company professionally.

---

# 4. Professional Conduct

Employees must:

* Conduct themselves professionally.
* Communicate respectfully.
* Support an inclusive workplace.
* Act honestly and ethically.

Behavior that damages workplace relationships or company reputation is unacceptable.

---

# 5. Respectful Workplace

InterCloud Solutions is committed to maintaining a workplace free from:

* Harassment
* Bullying
* Discrimination
* Retaliation

Employees are expected to foster a professional and respectful working environment.

---

# 6. Harassment and Discrimination

Harassment of any kind is prohibited.

Examples include:

* Verbal harassment
* Physical harassment
* Written harassment
* Online harassment
* Sexual harassment

Discrimination based on legally protected characteristics is prohibited.

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

# 7. Workplace Violence

The company maintains a zero-tolerance approach toward workplace violence.

Prohibited behaviors include:

* Threats
* Intimidation
* Physical aggression
* Destruction of property

Employees should immediately report concerns involving workplace safety.

---

# 8. Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests.

Examples include:

* Awarding contracts to family members
* Influencing decisions for personal gain
* Holding undisclosed financial interests in competitors

Potential conflicts should be disclosed promptly.

---

# 9. Outside Employment

Employees may engage in outside employment provided that such activities:

* Do not interfere with company responsibilities.
* Do not create conflicts of interest.
* Do not involve misuse of company resources.

Management may require disclosure of outside employment arrangements.

---

# 10. Confidentiality

Employees must protect confidential information relating to:

* Clients
* Employees
* Business operations
* Financial information
* Technology systems

Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

---

# 11. Intellectual Property

Employees must protect company and client intellectual property.

Work products created within the scope of employment belong to InterCloud Solutions unless otherwise specified by contract.

Employees may not use company intellectual property for personal gain without authorization.

---

# 12. Gifts and Business Courtesies

Employees may accept modest business gifts provided that the gift:

* Does not influence decision-making.
* Does not create the appearance of impropriety.
* Complies with applicable laws and client requirements.

Employees may not accept gifts with a value exceeding ₱5,000 without written approval from their Department Head.

Cash gifts are prohibited.

---

# 13. Fraud and Misrepresentation

Employees may not:

* Falsify company records
* Misrepresent business information
* Submit fraudulent claims
* Conceal material information

The company investigates suspected fraud seriously.

---

# 14. Company Property

Employees must safeguard company assets and use them responsibly.

Company property should be used in accordance with applicable policies.

Asset requirements are further detailed in POL-008 Equipment and Asset Policy.

---

# 15. Social Media Conduct

Employees should exercise professionalism when discussing company-related matters online.

Employees may not:

* Disclose confidential information.
* Misrepresent company positions.
* Share client information without authorization.

Employees are personally responsible for content they publish.

---

# 16. Client Relationships

Employees are expected to:

* Act professionally.
* Maintain confidentiality.
* Deliver services honestly.
* Avoid unethical business practices.

The company values long-term client trust above short-term gains.

---

# 17. Reporting Concerns

Employees are encouraged to report:

* Policy violations
* Ethical concerns
* Fraud
* Harassment
* Security concerns
* Misconduct

Reports may be submitted to:

* Managers
* People & Culture
* Information Security
* Executive Leadership

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

---

# 18. Non-Retaliation

Employees who raise concerns in good faith will not be subject to retaliation.

Retaliation against individuals who report concerns or participate in investigations is prohibited.

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

---

# 19. Investigations

The company may investigate suspected violations of policies, laws, or ethical standards.

Employees are expected to cooperate honestly with investigations.

Failure to cooperate may result in disciplinary action.

---

# 20. Disciplinary Action

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

* Coaching
* Written warnings
* Suspension
* Termination of employment

The company will consider the severity and circumstances of each violation.

---

# 21. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require approval from:

* Head of People & Culture
* Executive Leadership

Approved exceptions must be documented.
